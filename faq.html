Question 1: Who is eligible to register and play?

The Norwood Recreation Pickleball league is open for registration by adults, 18+, during advertised registration periods. All skill levels are welcome to join. The league is casual/recreational in nature!

Question 2: When is registration available?

Registration opens on Friday, August 25th at noon and ends on Monday, September 11th at noon.

Question 3: How do I join the Norwood Recreation Pickleball League?

Click on the Register tab and follow the instructions over there. It'll guide you through the process. The first thing you do is create an account. Once your account is created, you can register on the website for the Pickleball League (just search in the catalog). You may also stop into the Rec Center and register in person.

Question 4: How much does it cost to join the league?

The fee is $25/person ($50/team). You may register as a team and pay your fee together, or as a "Free Agent" and only pay your individual fee.

Question 5: What if I don't have a partner?

You may register as a "Free Agent" on the form. We'll place you with a good match partner to succeed! In this scenario, you would fill out the form by yourself and pay your individual $25 fee. Once you have finished registering, we'll add you to our free agent list and place you on a team.

Question 6: Do I need any equipment to play?

You will need to provide your own pickleball paddle. Paddles must comply with the USA Pickleball 2021 Pickleball Standards Manual There are many places to shop, both locally and online and most will advertise when they are compliant with the standards outlined. Here is a great guide in choosing a paddle that best fits your skill/play. That said, if you're unable to acquire a paddle, please indicate that on your registration form and we'll try to help! The league will provide standard pickleballs for all games. Other than paddles and balls, all you need is your best athletic wear.

Question 7: Where and when does the league play?

For the 2023 Fall season, league play will run Wednesday nights from 5:30p until 8:30p at the Norwood Pickleball Courts at Waterworks Park (adjacent to the parking lot that is down the hill behind the swimming pool).

In addition, there will be a Saturday morning Scramble event, from 9a-12p, during the season on September 23rd: registration to this event IS PUBLIC AND SEPARATE FROM LEAGUE REGISTRATION, however, Norwood Pickleball League members will receive advanced/priority registration access. More information on the Scramble event to come.

Question 8: What are the league rules?

League play adheres to standard USA Pickleball rules. Games will be played to 11, teams must win by 2 points. Please familiarize yourself with how to play beforehand. Here is a great introductory video on the basics of Pickleball. Reach out if there are any other clarifying questions you have. This league is casual/recreational in nature but fairness in competition makes for fun all-around for everyone.

Question 9: How does the league work?

The Norwood Recreation Pickleball League is a hosted by the Norwood Recreation Department and administered by the league's commissioner. For the Fall 2023 season, we will have 12 teams open for registration (24 players).

League play happens 6 straight Wednesdays, starting September 13th from 5:30p-8:30p at Waterworks park in Norwood. Each team will play three games against other teams in the league. The schedule is setup such that you will not have to be present the full time. Expect to be playing during a 1.5hour time window.

When registration closes, you will be assigned a Team number which will help you determine your schedule. The schedule will be posted on this website indicating each team's timeslots and courts. After the 6 weeks wrap up, the season will be concluded.

The season will also consist of a Saturday Scramble, however registration for this event is SEPARATE (it will take place 9a-12p on September 23rd). League members will get priority/advanced registration for seats in the Scramble event. More information to come on the Scramble!
